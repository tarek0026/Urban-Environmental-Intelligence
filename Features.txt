🏙️ City
Represents the name of the city where the data was collected.
Used for grouping, comparison, and merging datasets across locations.

📅 Date
The specific day of the recorded measurements.
Essential for time-series analysis, trend detection, and forecasting.

🌫️ PM2.5
Concentration of fine particulate matter (≤ 2.5 micrometers).
A key air quality indicator due to its severe impact on human health.

🌁 PM10
Concentration of particulate matter (≤ 10 micrometers).
Helps identify pollution sources such as dust, construction, and traffic.

🌬️ O3 (Ozone)
Ground-level ozone concentration.
Formed by chemical reactions in sunlight; indicates photochemical pollution.

🚗 NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide)
Emitted mainly from vehicles and industrial activity.
Strong indicator of traffic-related air pollution.

🔥 CO (Carbon Monoxide)
A toxic gas produced by incomplete combustion.
Important for monitoring hazardous air conditions.

🏭 SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide)
Produced from burning fossil fuels (coal, oil).
Contributes to acid rain and respiratory problems.

🌳 Green_Space
Represents the proportion or availability of green areas in the city.
Helps reduce pollution and improve air quality.

🌡️ Temperature_mean
Average daily temperature.

Influences both pollution levels and energy consumption.
🔥 Temperature_max
Maximum temperature recorded during the day.
Useful for analyzing peak electricity demand and extreme weather effects.

💧 Humidity
Percentage of moisture in the air.
Affects human comfort and the dispersion of pollutants.

💨 Wind_speed
Speed of the wind.
Plays a major role in dispersing or accumulating air pollutants.

🧭 Pressure
Atmospheric pressure level.
Used in weather analysis and can indicate stable or unstable air conditions.

⚡ Electricity Consumption
Total electricity usage for the city on a given day.
